What is Provident Fund?

Provident Fund (PF) is a savings scheme in India that is primarily meant for employees. It is a government-mandated scheme wherein employees and employers contribute a portion of their salaries and wages, respectively, towards a fund. The fund is managed by the Employees’ Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO), which is a statutory body under the Ministry of Labour and Employment, Government of India.

What are the types of PF?

  • Employee Provident Fund
  • Public Provident Fund (PPF)
  • Voluntary Provident Fund (VPF)

What is the interest allowed on PF contributions?

8.15% p.a. for FY 2022-2023.

Is the interest on PF taxable? How can 3G PF Registration Consultancy help you?

Yes, the interest is taxable at the applicable slab rate. Any interest earned on a contribution over and above INR 2.5 lakhs is taxable in the hands of the employees.

What is UAN?

UAN is a 12-digit unique number provided to each employee contributing to the employee provident fund.
